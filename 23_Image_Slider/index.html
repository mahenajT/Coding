Germany
Germany is known as the land of poets and thinkers, producing famous figures like Beethoven, Goethe, and Einstein.
Switzerland
Switzerland has four official languages: German, French, Italian, and Romansh, reflecting its rich cultural diversity.
Luxembourg
Luxembourg is one of the world's smallest countries but boasts one of the highest GDP per capita, thanks to its strong finance and banking sector.
Finland
Finland has the most lakes per capita in the world, with over 188,000 lakes scattered across the country’s landscape.
Iceland
There are no mosquitoes in Iceland, thanks to its unique climate and ecology—making it a rare, bug-free destination!
Greenland
Despite its name, Greenland has very little greenery—its name was actually a marketing effort by early Norse settlers to attract more inhabitants!
Netherland
Flowers have their own secret language; in the Victorian era, each flower had a specific meaning, allowing people to send coded messages through bouquets.
Ireland
Ireland is home to over 30,000 castles and castle ruins, with some dating back to the 11th century, making it a haven for history lovers.
