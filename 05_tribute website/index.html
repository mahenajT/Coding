A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

MISSILE MAN OF INDIA

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an eminent Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his crucial role in the development of India's missile and space technology, Dr. Kalam was a key architect of the nation's ballistic missile and satellite launch vehicle programs. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become one of India's most beloved scientists and leaders. Dr. Kalam's contributions extended beyond science and technology; he was a visionary thinker, educator, and a passionate advocate for youth empowerment and education. His inspirational speeches and books, such as "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," motivated millions of young Indians to dream big and work towards making those dreams a reality. His presidency was marked by humility, simplicity, and a focus on transforming India into a developed nation. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture to students, a fitting end to a life dedicated to education and inspiring the next generation. He remains a symbol of resilience, innovation, and hope for millions of people in India and around the world.
